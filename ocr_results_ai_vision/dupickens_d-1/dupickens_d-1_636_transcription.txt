Negroes, consisting of three Neg. Eliza about four years old, Green about two years old
and Billy about five years, also two beds, bedding &c, one horse, wagon and property
of every description, to have and to hold the said Eight and Cattle, together with all and singular
the rights, members and appurtenances to the said Negroes belonging or
in anywise incident or appertaining to the only proper use, benefit and behoof of the said
Henry S. Boggs, his heirs and assigns forever, by these presents and the said Elizabeth
Jane Hamilton, do hereby bind myself, my heirs, executors and administrators, to warrant and
title of the property from myself, my heirs, executors and administrators, and from all
other persons claiming or to claim the same or any part thereof. In witness whereof
I have hereunto set my hand and seal this seventh day of January
in the year of our Lord one thousand eight hundred and forty three and in the sixty
seventh year of the Independence of the United States of America.
Signed, sealed and delivered in the presence of us
Allen H. Steele, J. H. D. Runnels, Thos. McClure
Memorandum.

Jane Hamilton
Henry S. Boggs

South Carolina

Know all men by these presents, that I Jane
Hamilton of the State and District aforesaid for
and in consideration of the natural love good will and affection
which I have unto my son Henry S. Boggs of the same State
and District, other good causes moving me hereunto, have given, granted
and released, and by these presents do give, grant, release and confirm unto the said
Negroes, consisting of Negro Eliza, Antony about twelve years old, Antony about two years old
and Dick, together with all and singular the rights, members, hereditaments and appurtenances
thereunto belonging or in anywise incident or appertaining to the only proper use, benefit and
behoof of the said Henry S. Boggs, his heirs and assigns forever, by these presents and the said
Jane Hamilton, do hereby bind myself, my heirs, executors and administrators, firmly by these
presents, to warrant and forever defend the title of the property from myself, my heirs, executors
and administrators, and from all other persons or persons claiming or to claim the same
or any part thereof. In witness whereof I have hereunto set my hand and seal this
the seventh day of January in the year of our Lord one thousand eight hundred and
forty three and in the sixty seventh year of the Independence of the United States of America.
Signed, sealed and delivered in the presence of us
Allen H. Steele, J. H. D. Runnels, Thos. McClure
Memorandum.

Jane Hamilton
Henry S. Boggs

South Carolina

Personally appeared before me William Smith one of the Justices
of the peace in and for said District, J. H. D. Runnels and being duly sworn on oath said
that he did see Jane Hamilton sign, the within deed of Negroes to Henry S. Boggs of
the same State and District and did see Allen H. Steele and Thomas McClure
duly subscribe their names as witnesses to the same, sworn to and subscribed before me
this 25th day of April 1843. Winnsboro, S. C.
J. H. D. Runnels
