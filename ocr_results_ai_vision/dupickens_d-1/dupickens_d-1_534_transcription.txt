William Crenshaw

to

A. B. Grant

508

State of South Carolina, To all whom these presents may
concern William Crenshaw in the State aforesaid
send Greeting. Whereas I the said William Crenshaw in
my certain bond or obligation bearing date the Thirtieth
day of February in the year of our Lord one thousand Eighty two, stand firmly held and bound
unto A. B. Grant in the penal sum of Two hundred & fifty dollars, conditioned for the payment
of the just and full sum of One hundred and twenty five dollars lawful money of the
said State aforesaid, with lawful interest for the same from the date thereof, and for the
more fully appear. Now Know all men by these presents, that I, William Crenshaw am
indebted to the said A. B. Grant in the sum of one hundred and twenty five dollars, being the principal
sum of the aforesaid bond or obligation, for the better securing the payment of the said
sum of one hundred and twenty five dollars, according to the condition of the said Bond; and also in
consideration of the further sum of one dollar to me in hand paid by the said A. B. Grant at or before the
sealing and delivery of these presents, the receipt whereof is hereby acknowledged, have granted, bargained, sold
and released, and by these presents do grant, bargain, sell and release unto the said A. B. Grant
all that plantation or tract of Land containing two hundred
acres, more or less, situate, lying & being in Richland District on the Wateree
Creek, known by Crenshaw's old place, bounded on the North by lands of John
Brown, James Maverick & J. Brown, on the East by lands of said Brown, together with all and
singular the rights, members, hereditaments and appurtenances to the said premises belonging
or in anywise incident or appertaining. To have and to hold all and singular the premises before
mentioned unto the said A. B. Grant his heirs and assigns forever. And I do hereby bind myself, my
heirs, executors and administrators, to warrant and forever defend all and singular the said premises
unto the said A. B. Grant his heirs and assigns against me and my heirs and against every
person whomsoever lawfully claiming or to claim the same or any part thereof. Provided always, and it is the true intent and meaning
of these presents, that if I the said William Crenshaw, my heirs, executors or administrators shall and do well and
truly pay or cause to be paid unto the said A. B. Grant the said sum of money of one hundred and twenty five dollars
on or before the first day of March next, with lawful interest for the same, then this present
indenture and the estate hereby granted shall cease, determine, and be utterly null and void. Otherwise to remain in full force
and virtue. In witness whereof I the said William Crenshaw have hereunto set my hand and seal this
thirtieth day of July in the year of our Lord one thousand eight hundred and forty two, and in the sixty sixth year of the sovereignty and independence
of the United States of America.

Signed, sealed and delivered in the presence of:

J. W. Brown, R. Beale.

William Crenshaw (Seal)

South Carolina Personally appeared R. Beale Esq. and made oath that he
saw William Crenshaw sign, seal and as his act and deed deliver the
within Mortgage for the uses and purposes within mentioned and that he with J. W. Brown subscribe himself
a witness to the due execution thereof. Sworn to before me this 1st day of July 1842.

R. Beale

Before me J. S. Heidt C.C.

Received the 1st day of July 1842 and examined by W. L. Heidt C.C. A.M.C.
