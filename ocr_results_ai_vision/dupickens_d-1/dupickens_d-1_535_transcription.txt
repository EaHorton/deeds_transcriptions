James Henderson of some place the following property viz one Negro woman Slave
named Milly about nineteen years of age, and two children one named Fanny about
three years of age, one named Mary about one year of age. To have and to hold all and
singular the said property together with the future increase of the said woman slave
Milly to the said James Henderson his heirs and assigns forever. Provided always
nevertheless and it is expressly declared between the parties to this conveyance
Smith his Executors administrators shall be entitled to pay or cause to be paid unto
James Henderson his heirs, Executors, administrators or assigns the sum of one hundred
one thousand and twenty dollars to satisfy the said Henderson his assigns or family
or children. Signed on this 28th day of March 1842. When the above sale of
sale and every article in the hereby conveyed shall cease and the Milly Fanny and
other increase in full force and virtue. Dated 28th 1842. James Smith
Test. William Robinson
The within Mortgage was given to secure the payment of a Note within this, is a
copy below.

Fourteen months after date we or either of us promise to pay James Henderson
Administrator of the Estate of Thos Henderson deceased a home the sum of one thousand
and twenty Dollars for value received this 28th of March 1842.
Test Eliza Robinson James Smith
William Robinson

South Carolina Before me personally appeared Mary Miller to me known and made
Richard District oath that she was present and saw James Henderson sign seal and
as deliver the within writing for the uses and purposes within mentioned
Sworn to subscribed this day of 1842

Josiah Powell
To
William Whitmore

The State of South Carolina
Know all men by these presents that I Josiah Power of
Richard District in the State aforesaid for and in consideration
of the sum of Seventy Dollars to me in hand paid by the said
William of Richard District and State aforesaid the receipt whereof is hereby
acknowledged, and by these presents do grant bargain and sell unto the said
William Whitmore all that certain parcel or tract of land situate in Richard District
and State aforesaid, said lying on both sides of the north Fork of Little River, originally
granted to Thomas by beginning at a stake on the Ferry branch and running
thence south to a Chesnut station thence to a light corner thence to a pine corner
thence to a conditional line made by Thomas Fields and Thomas State to Little River
thence up Little River to a stake on the goods thence to a post oak thence to a pine
thence to the conditional line line of State thence to a stake corner on the south line
of the original tract to the place of beginning containing one hundred and fifty
acres more or less said Whitmore and I forever. Together with all and singular
the rights members hereditaments and appurtenances thereunto belonging or in any
wise incident or appertaining. To have and to hold all and singular the said premises
unto the said William Whitmore his heirs and assigns forever. And I do hereby bind
myself my heirs executors and administrators to warrant and forever defend all and
singular the said premises unto the said William Whitmore his heirs and assigns
against myself and my heirs and against every person whomsoever lawfully claiming
or to claim the same or any part thereof. Witness my hand and seal this 28th day of March
in the year of our Lord one thousand eight hundred and forty two and in the sixty sixth
year of the Independence of the United States of America.
Sealed and delivered in the presence of Josiah Powell (Seal)
