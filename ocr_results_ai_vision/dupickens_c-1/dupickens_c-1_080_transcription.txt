Benjamin Cooper
to
Alexander Sloan

Know all men by these presents that I Benjamin Cooper of
Pendleton District in the state of South Carolina for and in
consideration of the sum of fifty five dollars to me
in hand paid by Alexander Sloan of Greenville District in the state
aforesaid have granted bargained sold and released and by these presents
do grant bargain sell and release unto the said Alexander Sloan all that
tract of land lying being in the District aforesaid on the N.E. side of
George's Creek of Saluda River containing one hundred and four acres more or
less beginning at a hickory running thence N 88 E 10 chains 50 links to a stake
thence S 80 W 12.89 to a red oak, thence S 32 E 5.50 to a stake, thence the branch
of George, Creek, thence running down the said Creek with its meanders to a stake
thence S 45 E 8.50 to a white oak, thence N 88 E 8.50 chains to the beginning
corner on the forest fork of George's Creek, being the same land on the South
by lands of John Archer. On the North by the main fork of George's Creek.
Together with all and singular the rights members hereditaments and appurtenances
to the said premises belonging or in anywise incident or appertaining. To have
and to hold all and singular the premises before mentioned unto the said
Alexander Sloan his heirs and assigns forever. And I do hereby bind myself my
heirs executors and administrators to warrant and forever defend all and singular
the said premises unto the said Alexander Sloan his heirs and assigns against
myself my heirs and against every other person or persons whomsoever lawfully
claiming or to claim the same or any part thereof. In witness whereof I have
hereunto set my hand and seal this twenty fourth day of November in the year of our Lord one
thousand eight hundred and twenty two.
Signed sealed and delivered in the presence of } Benjamin Cooper (Seal)
John Archer, Matthew Norris, R. L. Cooper
